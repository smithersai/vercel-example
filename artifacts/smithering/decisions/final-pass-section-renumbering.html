Renumber sections contiguously (draft §3.5–3.7, §7.5 → final §4–6, §11); decision IDs untouched

Final design pass, 2026-07-01 · docs/planning/02-design.md · orchestrator judgment call

What was decided

The draft accreted review-driven sections as fractional numbers (§3.5, §3.6, §3.7, §7.5) so v1 section references stayed valid during review. The final doc is the durable artifact implementers read, so sections are renumbered into a contiguous 1–14 sequence. Decision IDs (D1–D31) are the stable cross-reference currency and are not renumbered; the doc header states the mapping.

Alternatives considered

A. Keep fractional numbering in the final doc Zero-diff and preserves draft section references, but a finished doc opening with "§3.5" signals patchwork and makes the execution architecture — the core of the design — look like an afterthought bolted onto §3. Rejected for a document meant to be a flagship example.
B. Contiguous renumber + explicit mapping in the header (chosen) Readers of the final doc get a clean structure; anyone tracing a draft-era reference has the mapping in the header and, more importantly, decision IDs — the unit review comments actually cite — are unchanged.

Example

Draft cross-reference "see §7.5 (coverage)" becomes "§11" in the final doc; a review comment saying "D29 needs seeded data" still resolves to the same per-Preview-database decision in both documents.
