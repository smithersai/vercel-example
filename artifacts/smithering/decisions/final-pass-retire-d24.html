Retire decision ID D24 (fold into D26) rather than renumber or backfill

Final design pass, 2026-07-01 · docs/planning/02-design.md · orchestrator judgment call

What was decided

The approved draft contained a placeholder: **Decision D24 — see also D26 (D8 chunking's crash-resume interaction).** — a stub with no content of its own; the substance (per-chunk delivery ledger) lives entirely in D26. In the final doc, D24 is declared a retired ID: a short italic note in §4 explains this, the Decisions index (§12) lists it as retired, and the ID is never reused.

Alternatives considered

A. Renumber D25–D31 down by one (D24…D30) Cleanest-looking numbering, but breaks traceability: the draft went through human review, and any review comment citing "D26" or "D27" would silently point at a different decision in the final doc. Rejected.
B. Backfill D24 with new content Inventing a new decision just to fill a number would be a substantive design change in a pass that is supposed to be polish-only on an approved draft, and anything true worth saying there (durable split, ledger skip-if-sent) is already stated in D26/D8. Rejected.
C. Retire the ID explicitly (chosen) Keeps every other ID stable, costs two sentences, and follows the common RFC/ADR convention of "number withdrawn, never reused."
