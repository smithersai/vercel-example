O1/O5 — One worktree per ticket; max 3 parallel workers

Context: 06-orchestration.md O1 & O5 · 2026-07-01 · target repo ., base main.

Decision

.smithers/worktrees/<runId>/<ticketId>   ← jj/git worktree (gitignored)
branch: smithering/<runId>/<ticketId>    ← forked from main head at dispatch
dispatch rule: all deps LANDED on main (not just "worker done")
resume rule: step zero = jj st / conflict check (auto-rebase may have "succeeded" past a failure)

Concurrency: 3 implementation workers + 1 merge-lane slot (O2). Reviewer/verifier agents attach to their ticket's slot.

Dependency-graph frontier (why 3 is enough)

Ticket 1 (walking skeleton) ── everything else
  after 1 lands, dispatchable: 2,3,4,5,6,7,15   (frontier ≈ 7, but…)
  after 7:  8,9 ;  after 9,3: 10 ;  after 6,4,5: 11 (A13-gated) …
Effective width is capped by the serialized merge lane: with ~5–10 min
Tier-1 land runs, worker #4+ spends most wall-clock queued or rebasing.

Alternatives

OptionVerdict
3 workers (chosen)Matches usable frontier width under a serialized lane; 3 concurrent local-PG Vitest suites is what one machine sustains without flaky timeouts — and flaky gate evidence is a product defect here, not an annoyance. Stays inside the provisionally-probed Neon quota (A4/A5).
6–8 workers (frontier-max)Rejected: merge-lane queueing + rebase churn eat the gain; oversubscribed suites produce nondeterministic failures that poison red-before-green evidence.
1 worker (fully serial)Rejected: dep graph has real width; run duration roughly triples for no correctness gain.
Shared worktree / workers on primary checkoutRejected outright: concurrent mutation of one tree breaks gate reproducibility and jj op-log hygiene.

Recorded tunable: raise to 4–5 if the lane is measurably idle and suites stable; no design change required.
